Help Expand the Game World

What is a Builder?

Builders are trusted players who can create and design new rooms to expand the game world. As a builder, you help create immersive locations, design interesting areas, and contribute to the shared adventure.

‚ú® Builder Benefits

  • Create your own custom rooms and locations
  • Design unique areas for other players to explore
  • Contribute to the game's lore and world-building
  • See your creations come to life
  • Special recognition for your contributions

Getting Builder Status

Builder permissions are granted by game administrators to trusted, active players who demonstrate:

  • Creativity: Good ideas for locations and areas
  • Writing Skills: Ability to write engaging descriptions
  • Game Knowledge: Understanding of the game world and lore
  • Reliability: Active participation and good standing
How to Request: Message an admin in-game or demonstrate your interest by suggesting room ideas. Admins may grant builder status to promising players.

Builder Commands

üìê buildroom [id] [name]

Create a new room with a unique ID and name.

buildroom my-cottage My Cozy Cottage
  • ID Rules: Lowercase letters, numbers, hyphens only
  • Must be unique: No duplicate room IDs
  • Good IDs: dark-forest, elven-temple, mystic-shop
  • Bad IDs: Room1, MyRoom, AWESOME_PLACE
Note: Newly created rooms are marked as "pending" and require admin approval before becoming accessible.

üìù editdesc [description]

Edit the description of the current room (must be a room you created).

editdesc A warm and inviting cottage with wooden beams overhead and a crackling fireplace. Sunlight streams through the windows.
  • Write 2-4 sentences for best effect
  • Include sensory details (sight, sound, smell)
  • Set the mood and atmosphere
  • Mention notable features players can examine

üö™ addexit [direction] [room-id]

Add an exit connecting to another room.

addexit north forest-clearing
addexit south city-square

Valid Directions:

  • Cardinal: north, south, east, west
  • Vertical: up, down
  • Diagonal: northeast (ne), northwest (nw), southeast (se), southwest (sw)
Tip: Create two-way connections! If you go north from room A to room B, add a south exit in room B back to room A.

üîí removeexit [direction]

Remove an exit from the current room.

removeexit north

üîç adddetail [keyword] [description]

Add an examinable detail that players can look at closely.

adddetail fireplace A warm fire crackles invitingly in the stone hearth, casting dancing shadows on the walls.
adddetail window Through the glass you can see the forest outside, peaceful and serene.
  • Add 3-5 details per room
  • Mention details in your main description
  • Reward player curiosity
  • Good keywords: fireplace, window, painting, statue, altar

üìã builderrooms

List all rooms you've created and their approval status.

builderrooms

Shows:

  • [PENDING] - Waiting for admin approval
  • [APPROVED] - Live and accessible to players

Building Workflow

Step 1: Create the Room

buildroom mystic-grove Mystic Grove

Room is created and marked as pending approval.

Step 2: Write the Description

editdesc Ancient trees tower overhead, their branches forming a natural cathedral. Soft, ethereal light filters through the canopy, and the air hums with magical energy. A circle of standing stones marks the center of the grove.

Step 3: Add Details

adddetail trees These ancient oaks are centuries old, their bark covered in glowing runes.
adddetail stones The standing stones are arranged in a perfect circle, each carved with mystical symbols.
adddetail light The light seems to come from everywhere and nowhere, neither sun nor moon.

Step 4: Wait for Approval

An admin will review your room using the reviewrooms command. If approved, you'll receive an in-game notification!

Step 5: Connect to the World

Once approved, navigate to a connected location and add an exit:

go forest-path
addexit west mystic-grove

Don't forget the reverse exit!

go west
addexit east forest-path

Writing Great Descriptions

The Formula

  1. Opening: Overall impression and dominant feature
  2. Details: Sensory information and atmosphere
  3. Atmosphere: Mood and feeling of the location
  4. Notable Features: Things players can interact with

Example: Dark Forest

‚ùå Bad Description:

This is a dark forest. There are trees here. It's scary.

‚úÖ Good Description:

The trees grow so close together here that barely any light reaches the forest floor. Strange sounds echo through the darkness - rustling leaves, snapping twigs, and distant howls. The air is cold and damp, heavy with the scent of moss and decay. Ancient oaks loom overhead like silent sentinels.

Tips for Descriptions

  • Use all five senses: sight, sound, smell, touch, taste
  • Show, don't tell: "cold wind bites your skin" > "it's cold"
  • Create atmosphere with word choice
  • Mention 2-3 examinable details in the description
  • Keep it between 2-4 sentences (50-100 words)
  • Match the tone to the location

Best Practices

‚úÖ DO

  • Create coherent areas (group related rooms)
  • Think about logical geography
  • Make two-way connections
  • Write vivid, immersive descriptions
  • Add interesting examinable details
  • Consider the player experience
  • Match the game's tone and lore
  • Proofread before submitting

‚ùå DON'T

  • Create disconnected random rooms
  • Make one-way exits without good reason
  • Copy descriptions from other sources
  • Use modern references or anachronisms
  • Create maze-like confusing layouts
  • Add offensive or inappropriate content
  • Make rooms just to increase room count
  • Rush - take time to craft quality

Permissions & Restrictions

What Builders CAN Do:

  • ‚úÖ Create unlimited new rooms
  • ‚úÖ Edit rooms they created
  • ‚úÖ Add exits to their own rooms
  • ‚úÖ Add details to their own rooms
  • ‚úÖ View all their created rooms

What Builders CANNOT Do:

  • ‚ùå Edit other players' rooms
  • ‚ùå Add NPCs or monsters to rooms
  • ‚ùå Add items to rooms
  • ‚ùå Approve their own rooms
  • ‚ùå Delete rooms once created
  • ‚ùå Add exits to pending (unapproved) rooms
Need More? If you need to add NPCs, monsters, or items to your rooms, or make other advanced changes, ask an admin for help!

Approval Process

What Admins Review:

  • Quality: Well-written, engaging descriptions
  • Appropriateness: No offensive or rule-breaking content
  • Coherence: Fits the game world and lore
  • Balance: Not exploitative or game-breaking
  • Spelling/Grammar: Clean, professional writing

Timeline:

  • Most rooms reviewed within 24 hours
  • You'll receive an in-game notification
  • Approved rooms become immediately accessible
  • Rejected rooms are deleted with notification

If Rejected:

  • Don't take it personally - admins want quality
  • Ask for feedback to improve
  • Revise and try again
  • Learn from approved examples

Troubleshooting

Common Issues:

Q: "You need builder permissions"

A: Ask an admin to run makebuilder YourName

Q: "Room ID already exists"

A: Choose a different, unique ID. Try adding numbers: cottage-1, cottage-2

Q: "Cannot add exits to pending rooms"

A: Wait for admin approval first. Once approved, you can add exits.

Q: "You can only edit rooms you created"

A: This is by design. You can only modify your own rooms. Ask an admin to edit others.

Q: "Target room doesn't exist"

A: Check the room ID spelling. Use builderrooms to see your room IDs.

Builder Etiquette

  • Quality over Quantity: One great room > ten mediocre ones
  • Respect the Lore: Stay true to the game's theme and world
  • Collaborate: Talk to other builders, share ideas
  • Accept Feedback: Learn from admin suggestions
  • Give Credit: Acknowledge inspiration when appropriate
  • Be Patient: Admins review in their spare time
  • Have Fun: Building should be enjoyable!
